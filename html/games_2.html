What I've Worked On

I don't have a lot of games still up from my past work, but I have completed three "demo-like" games (At least in terms of content). I never really got them to a state I fully liked, but they were finished projects nonetheless. The only one that is still accessible (at least without a direct link) is one called "Blank is the Fake" and can by found on my Itch page

Here's a list of everything I've done:


Feed the Beast - April 2020 | Link

Feed the Beast was my first released game, which was result of my first game jam: Ludum Dare 46. It was a simple little puzzle game where you had to run across the dungeon floor, completing various minigames to gather and craft items to feed the beast. I made the logo (shown on the left) when I was still learning pixel art. It was heavily based on an old logo from a group named Feed the Beast (I know, very creative). I pulled a few all-nighters in the process of making this game, and I still have a lot of good memories from it. Right now, I'm a little embarrassed of it and I think that's because I have built a higher standard for myself. But regardless, it was a good starting point for me!




Spiritual Connection - Jan 2021 | Link

Spiritual Connection was the second game I released, and it was my entry for Vimlark's VimJam game jam. This game was pretty special to me because one of my best friends actually made some music for it (He's Lenom on SoundCloud)! It was a simple little platformer where you had to traverse through the level to a "portal", which would then swap some objects around. The main idea behind this game was based on the P-switch from Super Mario. After reaching this portal, you would then have to go back through the level to the beginning with the new objects. I was only able to design 4 levels, and they are not balanced very well. However, it was still something I've completed and I think I improved a lot in art and desgin. But there is still a lot of work to do and improvements to make.




Blank is the Fake - Mar 2022 | Link

Blank is the Fake was the latest game I've released. I created it for the Brackeys Game Jam 2022.1. This one was by far the least polished and fleshed-out. However, it still has a place in my heart. I made a lot of design choices that I really liked (for example, all the transitions were "smooth" with no "fade to black" transitions). It is a puzzle game based on a mixture of different puzzles found in my two favorite Legend of Zelda Games: Phantom Hourglass and Spirit Tracks. The end product wasn't bad, and I did have plans to expand it past the jam's deadline. However, as it common with most of my projects, I quickly lost interest and moved on.

What I'm Working On

It's honestly been a little hard for me to focus on game development recently. And I can't even blame it on school or friends or really anything like that. I have plenty of time to work on it, especially late at night. However, I cannot seem to stay focused on a single game for an extended period of time. The longest I've really worked on a game recently is like a week or so.

Besides game development, I do enjoy creating pixel art (See Gallery). I've always loved the look of it, and I think I have gotten pretty decent. There are some pieces that I'm really proud of. And I think it's so interesting to see where I've come from and where I am now. But that's besides the point. The main thing I want to talk about is what I've been doing, and what I plan on doing in the future.

My main goal right now is to improve my skills with the Godot Game Engine. I have recently switched over from Unity, but I'm still learning the finer details of Godot as well as it's workflow. To do this, I am planning on doing something called the 20 Games Challenge. I already have a decent start with a Pong game, but I am again stuck in this loop where I work for a good few days, then lose interest or motivation. However, I think I'm really going to start pushing myself to work on this. I think if I can at least get through a few of these games, I can start to get used to finishing projects as well as learn Godot to a much better degree than I do now.

Game Launcher

Other than that, there is not a whole lot in the works. One thing I really want to work on is creating a game launcher that will download games from a GitHub repository that will automatically update when new versions are released. My plans right now are heavily based on the Minecraft Launcher. I have created a few version before this, and they worked pretty well, but they did not fully live up to my expectations. I think once I get a good selection of games, I might take a break to build this launcher. This could also help with testing if I can find a few people interested. Instead of sending them a link or file, I can just tell them to open the launcher and update the game. Idk how feasible this all is, but it's something I'm pretty excited to start working on
